# Mia Hamm

**Born March 17, 1972**

Mariel Margaret Hamm is a retired American soccer player, two-time FIFA Women's World Cup champion, and two-time Olympic gold medalist. She was the face of women's soccer for a generation.

International career: 158 goals in 276 appearances for the United States—held the record for international goals (male or female) until 2013.

Won World Cup titles in 1991 and 1999. The '99 World Cup final against China at the Rose Bowl drew over 90,000 fans—the largest crowd ever for a women's sporting event in the US at the time.

Named FIFA World Player of the Year twice. Inducted into the National Soccer Hall of Fame.

Co-founded the Mia Hamm Foundation, raising money for bone marrow transplant research after her brother Garrett died from complications of aplastic anemia.
