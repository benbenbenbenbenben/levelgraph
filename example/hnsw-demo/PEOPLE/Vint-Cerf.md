# Vint Cerf

Vinton Gray Cerf, born June 23, 1943.

"Father of the Internet" (along with Bob Kahn). Co-designed TCP/IP, the fundamental communication protocols that underpin the entire Internet.

Their 1974 paper "A Protocol for Packet Network Intercommunication" laid the groundwork for how all computers on Earth communicate.

Worked at DARPA where TCP/IP was developed. Later joined MCI where he led development of the first commercial email system connected to the Internet.

Currently VP and Chief Internet Evangelist at Google.

Received the National Medal of Technology and Innovation, the Turing Award (2004, with Bob Kahn), and the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

Hearing impaired since childhood, Cerf has been an advocate for accessibility in technology.

Famous for his three-piece suits and professorial demeanor.
